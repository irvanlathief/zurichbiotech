BPOM Regulation No. 27 of 2022, updated by Regulation No. 8 of 2024, governs research-use imports of unregistered pharmaceuticals through the Special Access Scheme, applicable to any Indonesian address including Bandung.

BPOM and the regulatory framework for research imports

BPOM (Badan Pengawas Obat dan Makanan) is the Indonesian national food and drug authority. Any pharmaceutical without a domestic Marketing Authorization must use an approved import channel. For research, clinical development, and non-commercial scientific use, that channel is the Special Access Scheme (SAS).

Research peptides are not classified as narcotics or psychotropics under Indonesian law, so they follow the standard SAS pathway rather than any more restrictive route. BPOM Regulation No. 8 of 2024, issued alongside Government Regulation No. 28 of 2024 implementing Law No. 17 of 2023 on Health, introduced a digital processing track for SAS authorizations, reducing administrative turnaround for research-use import requests.

The SAS authorization sits with the importing entity, not the individual recipient. A researcher at a Bandung address receives a compound already covered by that authorization. Orders ship with a Certificate of Analysis; the COA guide covers what to check on receipt.

Climate and storage in Bandung

Bandung sits at approximately 770 meters above sea level on the Parahyangan Plateau, placing it in a highland valley with mean annual temperatures between 20 and 25°C. Nights drop to 15 to 16°C during the dry season (June to August) and daytime highs reach 33 to 34°C from September through November. Relative humidity stays between 81% and 86% year-round, with annual precipitation of approximately 1,840 mm. The city is cooler than coastal Indonesian cities, but its humidity is not lower.

That humidity is the primary storage risk. A 2015 stability study in the International Journal of Pharmaceutics (Srinivasan et al., lyophilized hormone peptide formulations) measured a 20 to 27% decrease in peptide concentration under 25°C and 60% relative humidity over 8 weeks, with particulate formation appearing in reconstituted samples. Bandung's wet-season humidity of up to 86% substantially exceeds those test conditions; even the drier July-to-August period runs at 81%.

Lyophilized vials go into a freezer at -20°C or colder immediately on receipt. Consensus recommendations published in Clinical Chemistry (Hoofnagle et al., 2016, Peptide Biomarker Consortium) specify -20°C as the minimum for long-term storage and recommend limiting freeze-thaw cycles; correctly stored standard lyophilized peptides remain stable for up to 3 years at that temperature. A full storage protocol, including freeze-thaw limits and compound-specific guidance, is in the article on lyophilized peptide storage in tropical climates.

Bandung's electricity infrastructure is generally reliable across the city center, Coblong, and the Dago corridor. The aging distribution grid in some peripheral kecamatan produces brief outages during peak wet-season demand, particularly between December and March. A small UPS on the research freezer covers these interruptions. The Parahyangan Plateau's afternoon convective storms, typical from October through April, can cause voltage fluctuations lasting one to three hours; this is the main grid-stability consideration for cold-chain equipment in outer districts.

Research environment in Bandung

Institut Teknologi Bandung (ITB), founded in 1920 and ranked 255th globally in the QS World University Rankings 2026, maintains its main campus at Jalan Ganesha 10 in the Coblong kecamatan. ITB's School of Pharmacy (Sekolah Farmasi) runs research programs in pharmaceutical chemistry, pharmaceutical biology, pharmacokinetics, and pharmaceutical biotechnology, and publishes Acta Pharmaceutica Indonesia, a nationally accredited journal covering all aspects of pharmaceutical science. ITB's Faculty of Mathematics and Natural Sciences (FMIPA) hosts biochemistry and molecular biology research groups with output indexed in the Nature Index for the 2024-2025 window.

Universitas Padjadjaran (UNPAD), founded in 1957, operates its main campus in Jatinangor, Sumedang Regency. UNPAD's Faculty of Pharmacy is one of the top three in Indonesia by QS subject ranking 2025, with doctoral programs in pharmaceutical science and a clinical biochemistry concentration at the master's level. UNPAD's Faculty of Medicine oversees clinical research through RS Dr. Hasan Sadikin, a national referral hospital in the Pasteur area of Cicendo District, Bandung. Clinical research units there cover metabolic medicine, dermatology, and tissue repair, matching the compound categories most frequently ordered to Bandung research addresses.

Telkom University, in Bojongsoang subdistrict of Kabupaten Bandung roughly 7 km south of the city center, runs biomedical engineering and health technology programs alongside its engineering faculties. Universitas Islam Bandung (UNISBA) in Tamansari kecamatan and Universitas Katolik Parahyangan (UNPAR) in the Coblong corridor maintain natural science and pharmacy programs. The combined university-and-clinic research ecosystem across Greater Bandung produces demand that spans both longer academic protocol cycles and shorter private-clinic research runs.

FAQ

Is it legal to receive research peptides in Bandung?

Research-use peptides enter Indonesia under BPOM's Special Access Scheme, which covers non-commercial research purposes. Peptides are not classified as narcotics or psychotropics under Indonesian law. The SAS authorization sits with the importing entity; researchers at a Bandung address receive a compound already covered by that authorization.

How should research peptides be stored in Bandung's climate?

Lyophilized vials go into a freezer at -20°C or colder on receipt. Bandung averages 81 to 86% relative humidity year-round at mean temperatures between 20 and 25°C, conditions that exceed the thresholds at which measurable peptide degradation occurs. Reconstituted peptide goes into a refrigerator at 2 to 8°C and should not sit at room temperature between uses.

What is the typical delivery time to Bandung?

Next-day delivery covers all 30 Kota Bandung kecamatan, Kota Cimahi, and the Jatinangor corridor in Sumedang Regency. Most Kabupaten Bandung subdistricts fall within the same or a two-business-day window. All shipments go cold-packed regardless of destination.

Does Bandung's higher elevation make storage conditions safer than coastal cities?

Bandung averages 20 to 25°C, several degrees cooler than coastal Indonesian cities. However, relative humidity of 81 to 86% is high year-round, and daytime temperatures still regularly exceed 25°C. Lyophilized peptides require mechanical freezer storage at -20°C; the elevation advantage does not remove that requirement.

Which Bandung areas receive research peptide orders?

All 30 Kota Bandung kecamatan receive orders, including Coblong near the ITB campus, Sumur Bandung, Bandung Wetan, Cibeunying Kaler, Buah Batu, and Cicendo.

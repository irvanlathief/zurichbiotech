# Source authority hierarchy

Citations are the single biggest signal Google uses to decide if a
peptide-research site is trustworthy. We never cite forums, supplement
vendors, or AI-generated medical sites. Every claim of fact links to
one of the tiers below.

## Tier 1 — Primary literature (always preferred)

These are the citation backbones. Aim for at least 2 of these per article.

- **PubMed** — `https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/` — the canonical
  biomedical literature index. Search by compound name; sort by
  most-cited or most-recent.
- **NCBI Bookshelf** — `https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/` —
  full textbooks (StatPearls is good for compound pharmacology).
- **ClinicalTrials.gov** — `https://clinicaltrials.gov/` — trial
  registry; use NCT numbers for phase / status / sample size.
- **EU Clinical Trials Register** — `https://www.clinicaltrialsregister.eu/`
- **PMC (PubMed Central)** — `https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/` —
  full-text open-access papers.
- **Nature**, **Cell**, **NEJM**, **The Lancet**, **JAMA**, **Science**,
  **Journal of Biological Chemistry**, **Endocrine Reviews** — major
  peer-reviewed journals.

## Tier 2 — High-quality secondary

OK as supporting context, not as the primary citation for a research claim.

- **Examine.com** — supplement research summaries; well-curated.
- **Cochrane Library** — `https://www.cochranelibrary.com/` —
  systematic reviews and meta-analyses.
- **NIH MedlinePlus** — `https://medlineplus.gov/`
- **WHO** — `https://www.who.int/`
- **FDA Drug Approvals** — `https://www.fda.gov/drugs/development-approval-process-drugs`
- **EMA** — `https://www.ema.europa.eu/`
- **BPOM** (Indonesia) — `https://www.pom.go.id/` — useful for
  Indonesia-specific regulatory framing in city articles.
- **Eli Lilly / Novo Nordisk / Pfizer** investor and press releases
  for trial milestone facts (cite as company press, not as science).

## Tier 3 — Technical references / databases

For mechanism, sequence, structure data.

- **UniProt** — `https://www.uniprot.org/`
- **DrugBank** — `https://go.drugbank.com/`
- **PDB (Protein Data Bank)** — `https://www.rcsb.org/`
- **PubChem** — `https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/`
- **CAS Common Chemistry** — `https://commonchemistry.cas.org/`

## Tier 4 — Acceptable for context / color, never primary

- Wikipedia — fine for orientation while researching, never cite.
- Manufacturer product pages — only when discussing commercially-available
  compound specifics (cite as "Manufacturer X states", not as a
  research finding).
- Reuters, BBC, Bloomberg — for trial milestone or regulatory news.

## Banned — never cite

- Bodybuilding/fitness forums (Reddit, Bluelight, EliteFitness, etc.).
- Supplement vendor blog posts (other peptide vendors, "TRT clinics").
- AI-generated health content sites (most "best peptides for X" listicles).
- Any site that doesn't disclose its authors or references.
- Press-release wires (PRNewswire, Business Wire) as standalone sources.

## Compound-specific research entry points

### BPC-157
- Search: `BPC-157 OR "body protection compound" OR pentadecapeptide`
- Core literature: Sikiric et al. (University of Zagreb) — gastric
  ulcer protection studies, tendon healing models. Most BPC-157
  literature is from this group.
- Caveat: nearly all studies are rat/mouse in-vivo or in-vitro;
  human RCT data is extremely thin. Articles must state this clearly.

### TB-500 / Thymosin Beta-4
- Search: `"thymosin beta-4" OR "TB-500" OR "Tβ4"`
- Core literature: RegeneRx clinical work (TB-500 wound healing),
  Goldstein et al. on Tβ4 mechanism.

### Retatrutide (LY3437943)
- Search: `retatrutide OR LY3437943 OR "triple agonist"`
- Core literature: Eli Lilly trial publications, Phase 2 NEJM paper
  (Jastreboff et al. 2023), Phase 3 trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov
  (search NCT numbers).
- Compare to: tirzepatide (Mounjaro), semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy).

### GHK-Cu
- Search: `"GHK-Cu" OR "copper tripeptide" OR "Cu-GHK"`
- Core literature: Pickart et al. — gene expression / wound healing
  studies dating to the 1970s; more recent dermatology papers on
  skin remodeling.

### MOTS-c
- Search: `"MOTS-c" OR "MOTS-c peptide" OR "mitochondrial-derived peptide"`
- Core literature: Lee/Cohen lab (USC). Mitochondrial-derived peptide,
  metabolic effects, exercise mimetic studies (rodent models, some
  early human work).

### CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin
- Search separately:
  - `CJC-1295 OR "DAC:GRF" OR "GRF analogue"`
  - `ipamorelin OR "GHS-R agonist"`
- Core literature: Datta-Roy et al. (CJC-1295 original), Raun et al.
  (Ipamorelin original ~1998 from Novo Nordisk).

### Semax
- Search: `semax OR "ACTH(4-7) Pro-Gly-Pro" OR "Russian nootropic"`
- Core literature: Russian Academy of Sciences groups (Skvortsova,
  Myasoedov). Most studies are Russian-language; cite English-language
  reviews and translated abstracts.

## How to verify a source before citing

Three quick checks:

1. **Does the URL actually load?** WebFetch it. If it 404s or
   redirects to a paywall splash, find another source.
2. **Does the abstract say what you want to claim?** Many AI-cited
   "studies" don't actually contain the claimed finding. Read the
   abstract.
3. **Is the journal real?** If you've never heard of it, check it on
   Beall's List (now archived) or look for an impact factor. Predatory
   journals are a citation trap.

When in doubt: skip the source. A 1200-word article with 3 rock-solid
PubMed citations beats a 2000-word article with 8 shaky ones.
